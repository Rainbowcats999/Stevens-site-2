General Information

Stevens Institute of Technology, founded in 1870, is one of the nation’s leading research-focused universities. Located in Hoboken, New Jersey — just minutes away from New York City — Stevens provides students with the perfect balance of a close-knit campus and access to one of the world’s most dynamic cities.

With an enrollment of over 8,000 undergraduate and graduate students, Stevens is recognized for its excellence in engineering, computer science, business, and applied sciences. The university is home to cutting-edge research centers in artificial intelligence, sustainability, and healthcare innovation.

Stevens offers small class sizes, a student-to-faculty ratio of 11:1, and a strong commitment to experiential learning. Through cooperative education, internships, and industry partnerships, students graduate with not only knowledge but also real-world experience.

Programs

Engineering

Stevens offers world-class engineering programs including Mechanical, Civil, Electrical, Chemical, and Biomedical Engineering. Students gain hands-on experience in labs, collaborate on real-world projects, and graduate with skills highly sought by employers.

Computer Science

From Software Engineering to Artificial Intelligence, our computer science programs prepare students for the future of technology. With opportunities in cybersecurity, data science, and machine learning, students are equipped for both research and industry roles.

Business

Our business programs emphasize analytics, innovation, and leadership. Students learn from faculty with real-world experience and benefit from Stevens’ strong ties to finance, consulting, and technology industries.

Alumni

Stevens alumni have shaped industries across the globe — from pioneering research in technology and engineering to leading Fortune 500 companies. Our graduates are CEOs, innovators, and changemakers.

With a strong alumni network, current students benefit from mentorship, career opportunities, and lifelong professional connections that extend well beyond graduation.

Tuition & Fees

Stevens is committed to providing high-quality education and supporting students with financial aid and scholarship opportunities. Below are the average tuition costs for different programs.

Undergraduate

$57,000 / year
Includes access to labs, research opportunities, and a variety of student resources. Merit- and need-based scholarships are available to support qualified applicants.

Graduate

$48,000 / year
Graduate students benefit from flexible course schedules, research assistantships, and career development opportunities in high-demand industries.

Online / Part-time

$20,000 / year
Designed for working professionals, our online and part-time programs provide flexibility without compromising on academic quality or networking opportunities.
